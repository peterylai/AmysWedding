Our Story

by Justin Danger Roberts

Amy and I met in chemistry lab at Princeton in the summer of 2004. Ironically, we had no chemistry at first. I was finishing up my third year of grad school and Amy was starting her undergraduate research. I'm pretty sure she didn't like me much when we first met due to my antics in lab, but through mutual attraction and sheer perseverance, we began dating a year later anyway.

Since then, we've seen some good times and bad, had some amazing experiences, had some near-breakups, gone through a couple of graduations and jobs in different states and countries, and done some traveling both separately and together. It took breaking up briefly in December 2011 to push us to realize that we really wanted to make a deeper commitment to each other. We were both miserable without each other.

Finally, roughly a year after getting back together, I asked her to marry me right after the ball dropped this past New Year's. I wanted to spend the entire year knowing that we would be together...and that eventually she would promise to obey me (my favorite vow). To my delight, she immediately said yes. That's the end of that story, and the beginning of another one...


The Proposal

by Amy Doesn't Lai

One of our favorite places to go together was a beautiful mini-golf course in New Jersey that, to our great disappointment, has been closed for the last few years. So, when Justin found a book that had miniature golf courses that you could play on its pages, he knew he had to get it for his proposal.

We had a low-key New Year's Eve by ourselves at Justin's apartment in our pajamas making ice cream sundaes and watching our new favorite tv show. When the ball dropped, I called my parents to wish them a happy new year. My dad said cryptically to me, "This should be a good year for the two of you." Not knowing that Justin had called him earlier in the day to ask for his permission to marry me, I found his statement rather odd. After I hung up, Justin said he had another present for me.

"Who gives a present for New Year's?" I thought. But when I saw the book, I knew it was the perfect gift. As I browsed through the book, I already felt as content as could be to have such a thoughtful boyfriend. Then I reached the last page and there it was:



How could I say no?

Hotel booking

Hotel rooms are available at the Chauncey (use this link to get the discounted rate). A few of the many other options nearby are the Nassau Inn, Westin, and Howard Johnson.

Shuttle service will be available to/from the Princeton train station and Palmer Square in downtown Princeton.

Out-of-towners

If you are flying in and planning to take public transportation, your best bet is to fly into Newark airport (EWR). From there, you can take the free airport shuttle to the Newark Airport NJ Transit station, where you can buy a ticket to the Princeton train station. Hop on the Northeast Corridor line toward Trenton, and get off at the Princeton Junction station.

If you are staying at the Westin, arrange to be picked up by the Westin hotel shuttle.

If you are staying at the Chauncey, take the dinky to the Princeton train station and arrange to be picked up by the Chauncey hotel shuttle there.
